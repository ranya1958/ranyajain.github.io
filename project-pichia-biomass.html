Effect of Glucose Concentration on Pichia pastoris Biomass

Exploring growth and inhibition across a gradient of glucose media.

Overview

In this project, I explored how different glucose concentrations affect the biomass yield of Pichia pastoris, a yeast widely used in industrial biotechnology. Using OD600 measurements over 24 hours, I tracked growth across media ranging from 2% to 30% glucose.

The results showed that beyond a certain threshold, higher glucose levels actually inhibited growth, likely due to osmotic stress and metabolic saturation. This aligns with the idea that “more substrate” is not always better in bioprocessing contexts.

The project sharpened my interest in sustainable bioprocess optimization and taught me a lot about designing controlled experiments, analyzing microbial growth data, and thinking about how lab-scale results might translate to industrial bioreactors.
