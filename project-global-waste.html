Global Waste Analysis

Country-level waste, GDP, population, and composition · Python

In this project, I analyzed country-level data on GDP, population, waste composition, and municipal solid waste (MSW) generation to identify key correlations and national-scale drivers of waste output.

Using Python, I explored how economic growth, demographic patterns, and material composition interact with total waste generation. The goal was not just to find correlations, but to understand how structural factors might inform better waste policy frameworks, especially in rapidly urbanizing regions.

Working with this dataset pushed me to think critically about confounding variables, uneven data quality across countries, and the difference between a visually nice plot and a result that actually makes sense in a policy context.

Details

  • Variables: GDP, population, MSW generation, waste composition
  • Tools: Python (pandas, seaborn/matplotlib)
  • Focus: national-scale drivers and policy implications
