Effect of Temperature on Chemical Recycling of Polylactic Acid

Green chemistry · PLA hydrolysis · ethanol–NaOH · colorimetry

This project was a hands-on investigation into how temperature influences the chemical breakdown of polylactic acid (PLA), a biodegradable polymer. Using a simple ethanol–NaOH solution and colorimetry, I tested lactic acid production at four temperatures (40 °C to 80 °C) to observe trends in PLA hydrolysis.

While the experiment had clear limitations—short reaction times, relatively coarse temperature control, and some measurement error—it taught me a lot about designing and adjusting protocols, dealing with error, and working with green chemistry concepts in real life.

Beyond the actual results, this project made me more interested in sustainable materials and how experimental chemistry plays out beyond theory: how practical constraints, messy data, and small design choices shape what you can conclude.

Details

  • Polymer: polylactic acid (PLA)
  • Conditions: 40–80 °C, ethanol–NaOH solution
  • Measurements: colorimetric estimation of lactic acid
  • Focus: temperature–rate trends, experimental design
