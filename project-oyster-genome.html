Visualization of Genome Misassembly in the Eastern Oyster

Synteny-based workflows to diagnose structural issues in Crassostrea virginica assemblies.

Overview

In this project, I developed Python scripts to generate synteny plots for the eastern oyster genome using linkage map positions. By comparing the order of markers along linkage groups to their positions on genome scaffolds, I identified candidate regions of misassembly.

I created complementary Circos and Sankey-style visualizations to show how segments of chromosomes aligned (or failed to align) between the map and the assembly. These views make it easier to spot scaffold inversions, translocations, and potential mis-joins that may require reassembly or manual curation.

The workflow sits within broader efforts to achieve chromosome-level structural accuracy for Crassostrea virginica and to interpret local adaptation signals in the context of a reliable genome.
