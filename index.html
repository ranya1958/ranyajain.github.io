About

I’ve been interested in marine and environmental science for a long time, but I realized early on that many of the questions I care about come down to patterns in data: how conditions change over time, how species respond, and where systems start to break down.

That’s what led me to combine Data Science with Ecology & Evolutionary Biology. Through coursework, independent projects, and lab experience, I’ve worked with climate and ocean time series, ecological survey data, waste and socio-economic indicators, and genomic information. Across these projects, I’ve been building a foundation in data cleaning, exploratory analysis, basic statistical modeling, and communicating results clearly.

Going forward, I’m interested in using these skills in applied settings — for example on teams working on energy, climate, or sustainability problems — while continuing to grow technically and learn from people with deeper industry experience.

Some tools and methods I’ve used

Python (pandas, xarray, matplotlib), R, basic SQL, time series summaries, simple autoregressive models, correlation analysis, and data visualization for reports and presentations.

Selected projects

A sample of research and course projects where I’ve used data to explore environmental and systems-focused questions. Each project links to a short write-up with more detail.

Climate · Time series

Ocean Memory Timescale Analysis

Analyzed CESM1 daily sea surface temperature data (1980–2020) using Python and xarray to estimate ocean “memory” — how long temperature anomalies persist. This involved detrending time series, fitting simple autoregressive models, and summarizing results across regions.

Systems · Waste & policy

Global Waste Analysis

Explored country-level data on GDP, population, waste composition, and municipal solid waste generation. Cleaned and joined datasets in Python, created descriptive plots, and looked for broad patterns and correlations to understand what drives differences in waste output.

View project
Marine ecology · Resilience

Kelp Ecosystem Depletion in Tasmania

Reviewed ecological and climate data on kelp forests, sea urchins, and lobsters along Tasmania’s east coast. Used summary statistics and correlation analysis to compare different management approaches (urchin control, predator translocation) and their potential to support kelp recovery under ongoing climate stress.

View project
Materials · Green chemistry

Polylactic Acid Degradation

Designed and ran a small experiment on how temperature affects chemical recycling of PLA in ethanol–NaOH solutions. Collected and organized absorbance data, compared conditions across temperatures, and reflected on experimental limitations and what they mean for interpreting results.

View project
Bioprocess · Microbiology

Pichia pastoris Biomass & Glucose

Investigated how different glucose concentrations influence biomass yield of Pichia pastoris using optical density (OD600) measurements. Organized time series data, compared growth across treatments, and interpreted why very high glucose levels can become limiting instead of beneficial.

View project
Genomics · Visualization

Eastern Oyster Genome Misassembly

Worked with linkage map positions and genome scaffolds to generate synteny-style visualizations in Python and other tools. Used these plots to flag potential misassemblies and highlight regions that may need further curation in the eastern oyster genome.
